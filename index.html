Scalability
Measured by transactions per second (TPS) and block finality times. Higher scalability allows for higher throughput but often sacrifices decentralisation or security.
Security
Resistance to attacks (like 51% hash power or Sybil attacks). Highly secure networks prevent malicious actors from rewriting transactions.
Decentralisation
Degree of independent node participation. Greater decentralisation prevents single points of failure and censorship.

Real-World Blockchain & Cryptocurrencies

Layer Classification Guide:
  • Layer 1 (L1) represents the base consensus blockchain that establishes ultimate trust, settles transactions, and hosts smart contract states.
  • Layer 2 (L2) is a secondary framework built on top of Layer 1 to scale transaction throughput. L2s process transactions off-chain and batch-settle them back to Layer 1, inheriting its high security guarantees.
